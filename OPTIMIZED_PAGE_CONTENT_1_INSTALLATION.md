### SECTION 1: WHAT IS HYDRONIC HEATING INSTALLATION?

**H2:** What Is Hydronic Heating Installation?

**Copy (180-220 words):**
Hydronic heating works by circulating heated water through pipes connected to radiators or underfloor tubing. A boiler or heat source warms the water, which then travels through the system and releases gentle, radiant warmth into each room.

Because heat is transferred through radiant warmth rather than blown air, hydronic heating systems Melbourne homes use often feel more comfortable and stable. The warmth spreads gently through the space and maintains consistent temperatures across your entire home—no more cold spots or temperature fluctuations.

Hydronic systems can be powered by several energy sources including gas boilers, heat pumps, or solar-assisted systems. This flexibility allows homeowners to choose a heating solution that suits their energy goals and household needs.

**Key Differentiator:** Unlike forced-air systems that lose 20-30% of energy through ductwork, hydronic systems operate at 85-95% efficiency, meaning more of your heating investment actually warms your home.

**H3 Subsection:** How Hydronic Systems Work

- Water is heated by boiler/heat pump
- Circulating pump distributes hot water through pipes
- Radiators or underfloor tubing release heat into rooms
- Return water travels back to boiler to be re-heated
- Thermostat controls system operation and temperature

**H3 Subsection:** Main Components of Hydronic Heating

- Boiler or heat source (gas, heat pump, or solar)
- Circulation pump (moves water through system)
- Pipes (copper, plastic, or composite material)
- Radiator panels or underfloor tubing (releases heat)
- Thermostat and control system
- Expansion tank (accommodates water expansion)
- Isolation valves and balance valves (system control)

**H2:** Hydronic System Types & Options

**Copy (220-280 words):**
Different hydronic systems suit different needs. We help you choose the right option for your home and budget.

**Gas Boiler Hydronic Heating**
Traditional and proven reliable. Gas boilers heat water rapidly and efficiently, making them ideal for quick response to temperature changes. Best for homes with existing gas connection.

- Most affordable option: $8,000-$12,000
- Reliable and proven technology
- Requires gas connection

**Heat Pump Hydronic Heating**
Modern efficiency leader. Heat pumps transfer heat from outside air (even in winter) to heat water for your home. Significantly more efficient than gas boilers.

- Higher upfront cost but lower running costs (20-30% less)
- Environmental benefits
- Quieter operation
- Pairs excellently with solar panels

**Solar-Assisted Hydronic Systems**
Ultimate efficiency. Solar panels pre-heat water during day; boiler or heat pump handles peak heating needs. Exceptional long-term savings.

- Highest upfront cost but best long-term ROI

- Lowest ongoing energy costs
